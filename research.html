Research

My work addresses the fundamental question of how physical surface phenomena — particularly thermal emission and geometry — can be jointly reconstructed from multi-modal sensor data at building and urban scale.

Current Projects
2DGS · Thermal IR · LiDAR
Physics-Grounded Thermal Radiance Field Reconstruction

We propose a 2D Gaussian Splatting framework augmented with anisotropic adaptive diffusion constraints derived from the heat equation. By decomposing the radiance field into anomaly and background components, we enable precise thermal bridge localization via a TV-regularized Poisson inverse problem. Multi-view thermal infrared images and LiDAR point clouds serve as complementary inputs.

Collaborators: Jingwei Zhu · TUM · KIT (Boris Jutzi)

3DGS · Urban Scale
Urban-Scale 3D Reconstruction with Gaussian Splatting

Extending Gaussian splatting methods to city-scale datasets, with a focus on geometric fidelity, scalable rendering, and integration with semantic labels derived from LiDAR. Work developed in collaboration with the MIT Architecture group and Cambridge Digital Twin project.

Collaborators: MIT Architecture · Cambridge Digital Twin

Semantic LiDAR Point Cloud Analysis

LiDAR-based geometry reconstruction combined with semantic segmentation and physical constraints. Developed in collaboration with WHU (Yang Bisheng) and Tongji (Tong Xiaohua) groups, with applications to building inspection and urban mapping.
